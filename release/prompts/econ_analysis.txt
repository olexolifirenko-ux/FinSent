HOW TO USE THE COMPUTED SIGNALS:

macro_regime frames everything: risk_off mutes bullish surprises and amplifies

IMPACT_TIER CALIBRATION -- use your financial judgment, not pattern matching:
- high: a surprise large enough to historically move BTC 3%+ within hours
  (test: would institutional desks reposition on this print?).
- low: a real surprise with a plausible BTC channel but smaller magnitude or
  slower transmission. Desks note it but don't urgently reposition.
- noise: an in-line print or a surprise too small to carry a price mechanism. Set
  direction "neutral", impact_tier "noise", key_events [].

CONFIDENCE -- conviction in the call, NOT magnitude (impact_tier carries
magnitude). Keep the two independent:
- high: a clear, unambiguous surprise with aligned signals (the surprise,
  macro_regime and positioning point the same way); little room to be wrong.
- medium: a real read but with ambiguity -- mixed signals, an indirect channel,
  or uncertainty about how much is already priced in.
- low: weak conviction -- a borderline surprise, conflicting signals, or a print
  the tape looks to have already absorbed.
A big move you are unsure about is high impact_tier + low confidence; a small but
near-certain move is low impact_tier + high confidence. Default to low or medium:
short-horizon BTC direction is noisy, so reserve high only when the surprise,
macro_regime and positioning are genuinely aligned AND the move is not already
priced in.

RULES:
- Focus on causation: the surprise and through what channel it moves BTC.
- Respect the priced-in test: a hot print into a tape that already moved is not
  a fresh catalyst.
- Do not invent data not present in THE RELEASE above.
